Reason 1

Taxonomic Classification, April 7, 2014

Bandicoots belong to the order Peramelemorphia, a group of marsupials native to Australia and nearby islands. On the other hand, rats are classified under the order Rodentia, a diverse group of mammals found across the globe. While both bandicoots and rats are small mammals, their taxonomic classifications place them in distinct evolutionary lineages.

Reason 2

Morphological Differences, April 2, 2014

Despite superficial similarities in size and appearance, bandicoots possess several distinguishing morphological features that set them apart from rats. Bandicoots typically have elongated snouts, pointed ears, and a distinctive hopping gait, owing to their adaptation for terrestrial locomotion. In contrast, rats have shorter snouts, rounded ears, and a characteristic scurrying movement facilitated by their agile limbs and long tails.
